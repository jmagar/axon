The optimization of Heap-Only Tuples (HOT) is possible when two primary conditions are met during an update:
1. The update must not modify any columns that are referenced by the table's indexes, including expression and partial indexes [S1], [S4].
2. There must be sufficient free space on the page that contains the original (old) row to accommodate the updated row version [S1], [S4].

When these conditions are met, HOT provides two optimizations:
* New index entries are not required to represent the updated rows [S1], [S4].
* Old versions of the updated rows can be entirely removed during normal operations, including `SELECT` statements, instead of necessitating periodic `vacuum` operations [S1], [S4].
