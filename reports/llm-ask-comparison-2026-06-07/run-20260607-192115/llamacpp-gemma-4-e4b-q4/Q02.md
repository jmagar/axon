PostgreSQL's heap-only tuple (HOT) optimization is possible under two main conditions when performing an update:

1.  The update must not modify any columns that are referenced by the table's indexes, including partial and expression indexes [S1], [S4].
2.  There must be sufficient free space on the page where the original row resides to accommodate the updated version of that row [S1], [S4].

When these conditions are met, HOT provides two optimizations [S1], [S4]:

*   New index entries are not required to represent the updated rows [S1], [S4].
*   Old versions of the updated rows can be completely removed during normal operations, including `SELECT`s, instead of necessitating periodic vacuuming operations [S1], [S4].
