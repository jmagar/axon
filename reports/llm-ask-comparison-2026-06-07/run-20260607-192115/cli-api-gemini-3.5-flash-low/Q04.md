Normalized answer (stored for JSON and follow-up sessions):

Sparse vectors are highly useful in domains and scenarios where many rare keywords or specialized terms are present, such as in the medical field where rare terms may not be present in a general vocabulary [S1]. While general-purpose dense vectors can struggle to capture the nuances of such specialized domains or miss specific technical terms, unique identifiers, error codes, and product names, sparse vectors target these via precise lexical matching [S1][S2][S4].

However, sparse vectors fall short when it comes to capturing nuanced relationships between words, such as the relationship between "king" and "queen" [S1]. They also fail to capture the overall semantic meaning, context, or relationships of a text, which dense vectors excel at by representing the overall meaning to identify contextually similar items even if the exact words differ [S2][S3].
