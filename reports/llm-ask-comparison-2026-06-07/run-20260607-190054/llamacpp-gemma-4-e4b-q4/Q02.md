The conditions under which PostgreSQL's heap-only tuple (HOT) optimization applies are twofold: first, the update must not modify any columns that are referenced by the table's indexes, including expression and partial indexes [S1][S4]; and second, there must be sufficient free space on the page where the old row is located to accommodate the updated row [S1][S4].

When these conditions are met, HOT provides two optimizations:
1. New index entries are not required to represent the updated rows [S1][S4].
2. Old versions of the updated rows can be entirely removed during normal operations, such as `SELECT`s, bypassing the need for periodic vacuuming [S1][S4].
